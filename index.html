About OnTik

OnTik is Indigenous street and training wear – created by mob, for mob, and open to anyone who respects the story.

OnTik – Indigenous street and training wear

OnTik is my clothing brand about how we identify ourselves – who we are as Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander mob, who we walk with and where we come from. It’s about culture, staying grounded and not forgetting who we are – the stories and songlines that follow us wherever we go.

OnTik is about more than just looking fresh – it’s about staying on your path.

Created by a local Arrernte man from Central Australia, OnTik was dreamed up with our mob in mind. Too often our people are wearing big overseas brands that don’t know our Country, our colours or our story. OnTik flips that – bringing those stories and that pride onto streetwear and training gear that actually fits our lifestyles.

Shirts, singlets, shorts, hoodies, crew neck jumpers and track pants are all printed on demand, meaning less waste and more flexibility for clubs, teams and communities. Whether you’re at training, at the shops or just chilling with family, OnTik is a reminder to stay “on tick” – staying ready, staying focused and backing yourself and your mob every day.

About Belly

I’m Nathan, also known as Belly – a local Arrernte man from Central Australia. My heart is in closing the gap for Aboriginal people, especially our young mob. Through my Walk The Talk program I share lived experience and create spaces for youth to feel seen, valued and safe to yarn. OnTik is a separate lane focused on apparel, but it carries that same heart for culture, community and our young ones.

  • Indigenous street and training wear made to be worn hard, not just looked at.
  • Print-on-demand – no big stock needed, each piece is made when ordered.
  • Room to grow into fishing shirts, work wear and more as the brand builds.
  • Built to give our mob something of our own to wear – not just overseas logos.
